# Ambiguity Patterns & Anti-Patterns

<!--
source: curated
type: ambiguity_pattern
tags: ambiguity, risk
version: 2026-03-24
-->

- Vague improvements: "improve UX", "make it faster" without metrics.
- Missing actors: unspecified user roles, services, or systems.
- Unbounded scope: "support all browsers/devices" without priority tiers.
- Undefined acceptance: no success criteria, SLAs, or thresholds.
- Hidden dependencies: integrations, data sources, or feature flags not mentioned.
- Temporal ambiguity: unclear deadlines, blackout windows, or rollout phases.
- Environment ambiguity: prod vs. staging vs. specific regions not specified.
- Data ambiguity: schemas, retention, PII/PHI handling not stated.
- Error handling gaps: expected behaviors for failure paths not defined.
- Ownership ambiguity: no responsible team or escalation path identified.
